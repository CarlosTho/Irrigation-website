Succulent Water Requirements Explained

To know how frequently to water succulents, the first question is to understand their specific needs when it comes to watering. Succulent plants store water in their leaves, stems, and roots, thus enabling them to form part of biomes where there is always some water shortage. That said, because of their capacity to hold water, succulents are also prone towards over-watering, which can cause root rot and other problems.

Factors Influencing How Often to Water Succulents

How to water succulents will depend on a few factors, such as:
  • Type of Succulent

    • Each succulent has its own water needs. For example, being a succulent cacti generally watered less than other succulents.

  • Climate

    • The climate in which you live will considerably impact how often you water succulents. Succulents may need water more often in a hot, dry climate versus cooler or more humid regions.

  • Season

    • If you know the time to water succulents, think about the seasons' change in the spring and summer growing seasons. Most succulents will need more water. On the other hand, they must be watered up infrequently during the dormant season (Fall and winter).

  • Soil Type

    • If you use fast-draining sandy water to a dimension, it will be water more frequently. Good drainage is essential if you want to avoid a situation where water pools and sits around the roots of your tree (which can cause them to rot).

  • Pot Type and Size

    • The type and size of your pot will affect how often you water your ice plant. Terracotta pots are porous and will allow for the quickest drying process, while less so in glazed or plastic pots; larger pots mean more soil volume needing moisture yet possibly longer between waterings.

    How to Determine How Often to Water Succulents

    To determine how often to water succulents, follow these steps:
  • Check the soil moisture

    • Check the soil moisture before you water it. So feel it with your finger about an inch deep in the soil. When watering, ideally, you should get a feel for the soil by pushing your finger 3cm into it. Vaguely, if it's dry at this depth, then go ahead and water. If the meat feels wet, give it a few more days and check back later.

  • Look at the Succulent

    • Take a look at your succulent. If you are still determining when to water your succulent, a wrinkled or shrunken appearance often indicates that it needs more moisture. If the leaves are mushy or translucent, you have likely over-watered your hedgehog.

  • Water on a Schedule

    • It is nice to know when you should water, and your Plant will thrive on a more consistent (with some flexibility) schedule. Most succulents should be watered every 1-2 weeks during the growing season and once a month or so in the dormant season.

    When watering your succulents, the frequency is only part of the equation. Similarly, they need to be watered correctly. Tips For Watering Effectively

    How to Water Succulents: Seasonal Guide

    To give you an idea of when to water succulents during the year, here is a seasonal watering guideline:
  • Spring

    • Spring is the beginning of the growing season for most succulents. During this time, they will need more water to support new growth. Water your succulents every 1-2 weeks, ensuring the soil dries entirely between watering sessions.

  • Summer

    • Succulents may need help during the summer since taking care of them in some climates (like hot and dry regions) is hard. Increased watering could mean up to 1-1.5 times/ week. Give them plenty of shade, too (to avoid overheating plants and drying out when planting).

  • Fall

    • Water new shrubs every two or three days and nothing else as the growing season winds down. Give your succulents a good drink every 2 to three weeks, and let the soil dry out even more thoroughly between waterings. The latter allows the plants to be prepared for their dormant period.

  • Winter

    • In the winter, most succulents go dormant and like only to be watered lightly. Suppose you are specifically interested in caring for the string succulent Daniel Sorensen. That means providing water once every 3-4 weeks or less (depending on where you live and indoor conditions). Ensure.com The soil must dry completely before the next watering.

    Indoor vs. Outdoor Watering

    Regarding the above tip, I position some succulents in direct sun for 6+ hours daily. Take A Look At Indoor And Outdoor Watering:
  • Indoor Succulents

    • Due to the controlled environment, indoor succulents require less frequent watering than outdoor ones. However, indoor heating and air conditioning can affect soil moisture. Check the soil moisture regularly and water every 2-3 weeks during the growing season and every 4-6 weeks during the dormant season.

  • Outdoor Succulents

    • Outdoor succulents battle all weather elements, so how you water them is much different than how you care for indoor plants. Outdoor succulents may need to be watered more often, 1-2 weeks, during the growing season in hot and dry climates. Cut the job to 2-3 weeks for cooler or more humid climates.     How to Revive Over-Watered or Under-Watered Succulents

    If you suspect your succulents have been over-watered or under-watered, here are some steps to revive them:
  • Over-Watered Succulents
    • Take the Plant Out of The Soil

      • Carefully remove the succulent from its soil and check its roots. Cut away any rotten or mushy roots.

    • Dry the roots

      • Leave the roots to dry out in an open basket or tray for a few days before replanting into fresh, well-drained soil.These include changing your watering schedule, starting to water less often, and ensuring the soil is dry between waterings.

  • Under-Watered Succulents
    • When a succulent like this is potted up, you must water thoroughly, send the hose into one side of the basket, and fill it until solid saturation soaks into its roots.
    • Soak and Dry

      • Use the soak and dry method to water deeply and then allow the soil to dry out completely before watering again.

    • Observe & Adapt

      • Pay attention to how the Plant responds and adjust as necessary to maintain optimal growth.

    How to Water Different Types of Succulents

    Each species of succulent may have specific watering requirements. How to Water Super Common Succulent Types
  • Echeveria

    • Echeverias enjoy a soak and dry technique. water well and wait until the soil is arid before watering again. In the growing season, water is used every 1-2 weeks and reduced to once a month during dormancy.

  • Aloe Vera

    • Only water about once per month. Every 2-3 weeks during the growing period, down to once a month every time it is dormant. Be sure the soil drains well; you do not want your tree sitting in water, which can cause root rot.

  • Sedum

    • Sedums can withstand low-water conditions and should be watered less frequently. Water*(Every Two to Three Weeks in Grow Season, and Every Four to Six Week Dormant) Take care not to water too much.

  • Haworthia

    • Haworthias prefer moderate watering. Water is used every 2-3 weeks during the growing season and reduced to every 4-6 weeks during dormancy. Ensure the soil is well-draining to avoid root rot.
