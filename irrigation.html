Understanding an Irrigation System in a House

Put a home irrigation system to water plants in a house - including lawns and garden areas through pipes, valves, sprinkles, or other devices. With the help of this home automation project, homeowners can keep gardens watered without having to turn on and off a hose manually.

Components of an Irrigation System in a House

Some parts of a home irrigation system include:
  • Controller

    • This is the nerve of the home irrigation system, where you can set up watering schedules and control different zones.

  • Valves

    • Valves control the water flow in different areas of an irrigation system inside a building.

  • Pipes

    • Some pipes supply the water from waterworks to different areas where valves and sprinklers are installed in a house. Pipes must be tough/heavyweight and galvanized.

  • Sprinklers and Emitters

    • These devices distribute water across the entire lawn or in a specific area of your garden. Sprinklers are used in vast areas, while emitters are helpful for particular plants found in the irrigation system of a house.

  • Backflow Preventer

    • It is essential to ensure that water does not backrub into the domestic plumbing system and for healthy purified water.

    • The home irrigation system eases the workload by implementing an automatic watering practice, which is less time-consuming. With an irrigation system in a house, watering will be done regularly, which accompanies healthy plant life.
